# FIBER — Curation Policy

## What we list

FIBER lists clothing made from natural and plant-derived fibers. Every product on the site falls into one of two tiers:

### 100% Natural
Zero synthetic fibers of any kind. The entire garment is made from natural or plant-derived materials.

### Nearly Natural
Up to 10% elastane or spandex by weight. The remaining 90%+ must be natural or plant-derived fiber. This tier exists because stretch is essential for activewear, and a small amount of elastane is the pragmatic tradeoff that makes plastic-free dressing actually livable.

---

## Accepted fibers

### Natural fibers
Wool, merino, cashmere, alpaca, yak, mohair, silk, cotton, organic cotton, linen, hemp.

### Plant-derived fibers (regenerated cellulose)
Tencel, lyocell, modal, viscose, rayon, cupro, bamboo lyocell. These are made from wood or plant pulp, biodegrade naturally, and do not shed microplastics. We treat them as equal to natural fibers for tiering purposes.

### Allowed in limited quantities (Nearly Natural tier only)
Elastane, spandex, Lycra — up to 10% of total fabric composition.

---

## What we never list

- **Polyester** — in any amount
- **Nylon** — in any amount
- **Acrylic** — in any amount
- **Polypropylene or other petroleum-based fibers** — in any amount

If a garment contains any of these, it does not appear on FIBER regardless of the percentage.

---

## Transparency

Every product on FIBER displays its full material composition with percentages. We don't hide the elastane — we show it clearly so shoppers can make their own informed choice between 100% Natural and Nearly Natural options.

---

## Brand-level classification

- **Fully Natural brand**: every product in the brand's catalog qualifies for FIBER (either tier). Examples: Naadam.
- **Mixed catalog brand**: some products qualify, others don't. We list only the qualifying products. Examples: Allbirds, Prana.
