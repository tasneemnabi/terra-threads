## Design Context

### Users
Environmentally conscious consumers — primarily women at launch — searching for clothing made from natural fibers. They arrive wanting to shop their values (no polyester, no synthetics) without sacrificing style or spending hours researching brands. They expect transparency: show them exactly what's in the garment. The site is an aggregator, not a store — it curates and directs them to the right brand.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, honest, refined.** Approachable but elevated — like a knowledgeable friend with great taste who happens to know every natural fiber brand worth buying from. Never preachy or guilt-driven. Lets quality and transparency do the persuading.

### Emotional Goals
- **Trust & calm**: Reassurance that they're making good choices — grounded, credible, no greenwashing
- **Discovery & delight**: Excitement about finding beautiful natural alternatives they didn't know existed
- **Editorial authority**: The feeling of reading a curated magazine — expert, opinionated, elevated

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm minimal + Swiss-editorial.** Clean typography with generous whitespace, subtle organic textures, and an earthy palette that feels human, not sterile. Rounded corners soften the Swiss grid. Transitions are smooth and understated.

- **Palette**: Warm cream base (#FAF7F2), dusty rose accent (#B5636A), cool slate secondary (#475658), natural green (#6B8F71) for eco-tier badges, dark brown text (#2C2420)
- **Typography**: Space Grotesk (display/headings — bold, geometric) + DM Sans (body/UI — clean, readable). Tight letter-spacing on headlines for a premium feel.
- **Theme**: Light mode only. No dark mode.
- **Anti-references**: NOT Amazon, NOT fast fashion. No visual clutter, no discount aesthetics, no overwhelming product grids, no shouting CTAs. No generic SaaS dashboards. No stats-driven hero headlines.

### Design Principles
1. **Transparency first** — Show material composition prominently (Fiber Facts label). Never hide what's in the garment. Full percentages, always.
2. **Warmth over sterility** — Cream > white. Dusty rose > corporate blue. Texture > flat. The palette should feel like touching natural fabric, not browsing a tech product.
3. **Editorial curation, not marketplace chaos** — Every screen should feel curated and intentional. Limit visible products per row. Group by brand. Let whitespace breathe.
4. **Quiet confidence** — No hard sells, no urgency tactics, no "X left in stock!" banners. Trust the user to make informed decisions when given honest information.
5. **Details matter** — Micro-interactions (subtle hover lifts, smooth transitions), consistent spacing, and typographic hierarchy signal quality and care.

### Accessibility
- Target: **WCAG AA** compliance
- Good contrast ratios (dark brown #2C2420 on cream #FAF7F2)
- Focus rings on all interactive elements
- Semantic HTML throughout
- Keyboard navigable
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations

### Tech Stack
Next.js 16 + React 19 + TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v4, Supabase, Vercel. Custom components — no UI library (no shadcn, no MUI). All styling via Tailwind utility classes.
